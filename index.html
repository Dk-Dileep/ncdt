NCDT 2023 Conference Highlights

Introduction

It's not too late, but I want to share some key points that touched me when I attended the 2-day 1st National Conference at Andhra University (NCDT 2023). This program was organized by Andhra University Transdisciplinary Research Hub.

The statistics of this particular event were impressive. They received more than 1516 papers, and after a careful selection process, they confined it down to 452 papers for presentation. With participation from about 40 departments, this conference truly showcased the essence of transdisciplinary research.

Parallelly, there were 33 tracks, which means there were 33 simultaneous seminars and conferences conducted at different spots. It was remarkable to witness over 1500 participants who were punctual and engaged. The credit goes to the dedicated organizing chairs, who ensured a seamless experience.

Session-1

It's great to know that the conference was graced by Prof P.V.S.D Prasad Reddy Garu, the Vice Chancellor of Andhra University. Having the Vice Chancellor present at the event demonstrates the importance and support given to such conferences by the university administration. Prof P.V.S.D Prasad Reddy Garu's presence would have undoubtedly added prestige to the conference and provided valuable insights and guidance to the participants.

During the session, Prof P.V.S.D Prasad Reddy Garu discussed the initiation of transdisciplinary research and its significance. He emphasized the importance of transformative technologies and how they are being talked about in various fields such as science, arts, engineering, and pharmacy. The term "disruptive technologies" is commonly used in conferences and seminars, highlighting the transformative age we are living in.

Prof P.V.S.D Prasad Reddy Garu highlighted that disruptive forces and technologies, along with the pandemic, have changed our way of functioning. Even in remote villages, people are now adept at using technology, whereas experienced teachers were initially hesitant to embrace it. He emphasized the need for continuous learning and how the expectations of the industry have changed due to transformative technologies.

He urged the participants to develop a sustainable environment and adapt their models to add value in this ever-changing world. Prof P.V.S.D Prasad Reddy Garu emphasized the importance of out-of-the-box thinking and the ability to capture value. He mentioned the need for national institutions to explore beyond government funding and to embrace new ways of capturing value.

He concluded by emphasizing the importance of asking better questions to find better answers and how transformative technologies have the power to change the world. He emphasized the need for continuous learning and adapting to the changing landscape.

SESSION -2 - Chief Guest from GVP Engineering College

Dr. Ravi Saripalle Gaaru, the Director of the Centre for Innovation and Incubation at Gayathri Vidya Parishad College of Engineering, has made commendable contributions to the field of innovation and entrepreneurship. Under his guidance, the center has successfully incubated more than 15 startups in the past 12 years, demonstrating his dedication to supporting and nurturing budding entrepreneurs.

Dr. Ravi Saripalle's leadership and efforts in promoting innovation and incubation have played a vital role in fostering an entrepreneurial ecosystem at Gayathri Vidya Parishad College of Engineering. His work has contributed significantly to the development and growth of startups in the region, as well as the promotion of intellectual property.

During his session, Dr. Ravi Saripalle Gaaru mentioned the introduction of a course called Computer Science 50 at Harvard University, which is considered one of the world's most renowned certifications. He highlighted that this year, Andhra University has introduced a similar course, CS 50 GPT, to cover topics such as science coding, bug fixing, and implementation in an end-to-end scenario.

Dr. Ravi Saripalle Gaaru discussed the future job prospects and mentioned that the world will offer only five types of jobs:

  1. Entrepreneurs who start their own companies
  2. Domain specialists and technology specialists who tune and train big massive models
  3. Prompt engineering
  4. Design thinkers
  5. Remaining AI users

He focused on design thinking as a centered approach for experiential innovation. Experiential innovation, he explained, encompasses human desirability, technical feasibility, and business viability. When experiential innovation is combined with human-centeredness, it is called design thinking.

Dr. Ravi Saripalle Gaaru differentiated between design and design thinking, stating that design is about how something looks, while design thinking is about how it works. He provided examples such as Vijay Shekar's Patm Box and the story of the baby incubator to illustrate the application of design thinking.

KEYNOTE SPEAKER - Mr. Sairam Bollapragada Gaaru

"Design Thinking - A Glue between Academia & Industry," Mr. Sairam Bollapragada Gaaru, the founder of EssEnnTech Solutions, highlighted the power and usage of design thinking in addressing various societal challenges. Here are the key points he mentioned:

  1. Literacy: Design thinking can be utilized to enhance literacy rates and improve educational systems.
  2. Poverty: By applying design thinking principles, innovative solutions can be developed to alleviate poverty and create economic opportunities.
  3. Hunger: Design thinking methodologies can be employed to tackle the issue of hunger by developing sustainable and efficient food production and distribution systems.
  4. Digital literacy: IBM Design Thinking was specifically mentioned, suggesting its potential in addressing digital literacy challenges and promoting technological inclusivity.
  5. Inequality: Design thinking can contribute to reducing inequality by fostering equal access to resources, opportunities, and services.

To implement design thinking effectively, Mr. SaiRam Bollapragada Gaaru suggested following the EDIPT framework:

  1. Emphasize: Giving importance to the problem or challenge at hand and understanding its significance.
  2. Define: Clearly defining and framing the problem to gain a deeper understanding of its complexities.
  3. Ideate: Generating a wide range of ideas and solutions through brainstorming and creative thinking.
  4. Prototype: Building tangible representations or models of the proposed solutions to test and refine them.
  5. Test: Conducting experiments and gathering feedback to evaluate the effectiveness of the prototypes and make necessary iterations.

These steps in the EDIPT framework can help foster a human-centered approach to problem-solving, bridging the gap between academia and industry to create innovative solutions that address real-world challenges.

PANEL DISCUSSION

Panelists: SaiRam Gaaru, Murthy Gaaru, Ravi Eswarapu Gaaru, Ravi Saripalle Gaaru

The panel discussion focused on the question of why research is important. Here are the perspectives shared by the panelists:

Prof H. Purushotham Gaaru: Research is a personal journey of education and self-development. It involves finding new solutions to existing problems and is essential for personal growth and understanding the world. All the benefits that society enjoys today in terms of science and technology are a result of research. Research should be considered a fundamental right for all citizens to encourage innovative thinking and better solutions for society.

Dr. Murthy Gaaru - ISRO: The world is vast, and knowledge is vast as well. Research is the process of addressing problems, filling gaps in knowledge, or discovering entirely new things. It is a means of exploration and discovery, where researchers act as voyagers, traveling to different parts of the world to gather information and contribute to the existing knowledge pool.

Dr. Ravi Saripalle Gaaru: Research allows us to explore the undiscovered parts of the world, which can be seen as different realms of knowledge. Researchers are like travelers who embark on a journey of discovery. They collect information, correct existing knowledge, and share their findings through various channels, such as blogs. Research is an essential process of uncovering new insights.

Shri. Ravi Eswarapu Gaaru: Research is not just a process; it is crucial for survival, progress, and success. It is through research that we make advancements, drive progress, and achieve success in various fields.

Special Person - Shri Mahesh Rakheja Gaaru, Director

During his session, Shri Mahesh Rakheja Gaaru discussed the contrast between the virtual world and the real world. He provided examples to highlight the influence and dynamics of these two worlds:

Example 1

In the morning, even for just a few seconds, he instinctively checks his phone for notifications and messages, despite knowing that it's unlikely to have any at that time. This behavior reflects the simultaneous existence of two worlds:

  1. The physical world, where people can see and communicate with each other.
  2. The virtual world, which offers the flexibility to change and control our online presence.

Example 2

During his engineering days, he shared a humorous experience where he jokingly posted on Facebook that he had failed two exams. His director questioned him about the failure, assuming it to be true. This incident made him realize that people tend to trust the virtual world more than the real world.

Example 3

He mentioned the impact of social media on personal perceptions. Changing one's birthdate on Instagram to the following day can result in receiving birthday wishes from others. This highlights how online expressions and content influence how people judge and perceive individuals even before meeting them in person.

He also addressed the automatic uploading of photos to Google and explained the role of spiders and crawlers in indexing and storing photos. This discussion served as an awareness about protecting our virtual world from potential cybercrime.

The session concluded with valuable interactions among the special guests and the speaker. Additionally, Shri Mahesh Rakheja Gaaru expressed gratitude for the insightful sessions with senior individuals, Prasanna Kumar, Rameez, Cherishma, Dharani, and his friend Tarun.
